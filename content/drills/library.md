# Drill Library

The practical companion to the [syllabus](../syllabus/README.md). Every named
drill in the sources is written here as a **drill card** with the same five
fields, so a coach can pull a card and run it. Cards are grouped by syllabus
area; jump to the area you're working and the relevant cards are together.

Two classes of card appear:

- **Named drills** — exercises the sources name and define directly. The
  Plan names most of these (the footwork sequence, the dive family, the catch
  family, the set-piece situations); each card preserves the source's own
  description verbatim-faithfully.
- **Technique-implied drills** — exercises that follow directly from a
  technique the sources teach but do not write up as a standalone drill.
  Robo ("The GK Trainer") is text-only with no drill section, so its
  ten techniques are turned into cards here without inventing new technique:
  the **Objective**, **Coaching cues**, and progression all come straight from
  Robo's technical detail. Where a card is technique-implied rather than named,
  it says so.

**How to run a card.** Every drill runs inside one of the two delivery methods
— **Simple-to-Complex** or **Whole-Part-Whole** — and always finishes at the
most match-realistic rep the technique allows (see
[training-methodology](../framework/training-methodology.md)). The Plan and
Robo agree the session shape is warm-up (technique basics as a staple) →
technique session → finish with a little decision-making or fitness. Younger
keepers get a technique emphasis; older keepers add positioning and fitness.

**Equipment.** Ample cones, markers, flags, and a goal. Cones/markers/flags
work best at the start of a session or for "pull-out" work; for the meat of a
session a full-sized goal is preferable to any other implement. Survey the
ground first — bumpy, hard, or uneven surfaces are unsafe and unproductive for
goalkeeping work (Plan, Robo).

**A note on service.** Most cards specify a served ball. Begin with hand
service or a light strike at the body, progress to driven balls, to balls
away from the body, and finally to live/match service with teammates and
opponents introduced — that progression is the Simple-to-Complex / Whole-Part-
Whole spine, not a per-drill invention.

## Footwork & Movement

Cross-links: see [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md) and
the [glossary](../vocabulary.md).

### Ready Position / Set Yourself

- **Objective:** Establish the static base every save is launched from — and
  the habit of arriving at it *before* the shot.
- **Setup:** Keeper on or just off the line, a server 8–12 yards out with a
  supply of balls. Cones optional to mark start spots.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Keeper holds the Ready Position on command
  and the coach checks the shape. (2) Keeper moves a short distance, then on
  the call "**Set**" stops moving and gets into Ready Position — the **Set
  Yourself** action — before the server strikes. (3) Server strikes only once
  the keeper is set; if the keeper is still moving at contact, no save counts.
  (4) Progress to the keeper choosing the moment to set as the server winds up.
- **Coaching cues:** Two hands down to the sides of the body; knees bent; body
  in a squat position; feet shoulder width apart; no movement, just attention
  to the ball. When ball/attacker are close (3–5 yards) or the keeper has come
  off the line, hold with the knees slightly bent and the hands low to protect
  a shot from going under the body (Robo). Mature keepers appear calm as the
  ball is hit — asked what they think about when set, a developed keeper
  answers "absolutely nothing" (Robo).
- **Syllabus area:** [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md),
  [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md).
- _Named in ECNL/Plan ("Goalie Stance / Ready Position," "Set Yourself");
  the holding detail is Robo._

### Stalk

- **Objective:** Maintain a low center of gravity and balance while tracking
  an opponent who has the ball.
- **Setup:** A dribbler (or coach with a ball) and the keeper, in open space
  in front of goal. No service needed at first.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) The dribbler moves slowly side to side and
  forward; in a **LOW Ready Position** the keeper keeps their feet and follows
  the opponent, never crossing the feet or popping upright. (2) Increase the
  dribbler's speed and add change-of-direction. (3) Add a shot or a beat-the-
  keeper attempt at the end so the keeper learns to stalk *then* react.
- **Coaching cues:** Stay low; the whole point is to keep the feet and hold
  steady balance during movement — this requires better-than-average footwork.
  Do not commit; stalking is the patient tool that sets up stealing ground and,
  later, the dive or smother.
- **Syllabus area:** [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md),
  [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL)._

### Steal Ground

- **Objective:** Cut the angle and shorten the distance to the ball against a
  dribbler, without getting beaten.
- **Setup:** A dribbler with a ball running at the keeper; the keeper starting
  from a set position off the line. Mark a start cone and a "ball away from
  feet" trigger zone if useful.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) The keeper steps toward the dribbler,
  **taking ground only while the ball is away from the dribbler's feet**.
  (2) When the ball returns to the dribbler's foot, the keeper sets again —
  steal, set, steal, set. (3) Combine with **Stalk**: stalk while the ball is
  controlled, steal ground on each touch that pushes the ball away.
- **Coaching cues:** Steps go forward **only once the opponent dribbles the
  ball away from their foot** — stealing ground while the ball is at the
  dribbler's feet invites the nutmeg or the easy beat. Each stolen yard cuts
  the angle and shortens the distance to a winnable ball.
- **Syllabus area:** [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md),
  [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL)._

### Lateral Footwork — Shuffle & Sprint

- **Objective:** Move across the goal while staying square to the field, and
  arrive *set* for the shot.
- **Setup:** Full-sized goal, server able to deliver to near and far post.
  Cones mark near-post, central, and far-post start points.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) **Shuffle** across the goal facing the
  field of play; no crossing of the feet. (2) For longer distances, **sprint**
  across the goal keeping the shoulders turned so the keeper always faces the
  field of play. (3) The key rep: at the end of the sprint, take a **step
  sideways to come out of the sprint** — this slows momentum and gets the
  keeper set for the shot coming onto goal. (4) Server then strikes the moment
  the keeper sets, so the keeper learns to finish movement *before* the shot.
- **Coaching cues:** First step is taken by the foot closest to the ball, as
  long as possible, adjusting to the serve; the far foot smoothly follows.
  Keep the body square to the line of flight. Strong near-post / far-post
  footwork is what establishes good angles.
- **Syllabus area:** [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo's footwork detail; shuffle/sprint terms are
  Robo's._

### Power Step

- **Objective:** Generate the lateral push toward the ball that initiates a
  dive.
- **Setup:** Keeper set in the goal; server delivers balls a stride to either
  side at catchable height. Low cones can mark the foot's landing target.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) On the served ball, the **foot closest to
  the ball steps laterally toward the ball** — isolate just this step, no dive
  yet, keeper recovers. (2) Add the dive off the step (feeds straight into the
  **Step Dive** and **Extension / Power Dive** cards). (3) Vary the side and
  height so the keeper power-steps off either foot.
- **Coaching cues:** The step is *toward* the ball, lateral, off the near foot
  — it is the spring source for the long dive. As the keeper pushes off on a
  full extension, the top leg also comes across the body; that across-the-body
  motion is the **power step** (Robo).
- **Syllabus area:** [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md),
  [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL); the across-the-body detail is Robo._

## Handling & Catching

Cross-links: see [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md) and
the [glossary](../vocabulary.md). The governing principle across every card
here: **catch with two hands first; parry or punch only as a last resort** —
a held ball gives no second finishing opportunity (Robo).

### Ball-at-Body Catch

- **Objective:** Catch a ball driven straight at the body and lock it into the
  chest with no rebound.
- **Setup:** Server 8–12 yards out; goal behind the keeper. Start with balls
  at chest/stomach, then low driven balls, then balls just above the head.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Get as much body behind the ball as
  possible. (2) Shape the hands behind the ball — **concave** position, fingers
  and thumbs cupped, hands pointed **downward on low balls and upward on higher
  balls**. (3) On contact, curl the ball into the chest. (4) For a ball into
  the chest, absorb it by **taking a little jump forward** as the ball is
  pulled in. (5) Progress to driven balls — the common failure is hands on the
  *side* of the ball rather than behind it.
- **Coaching cues:** Hands are the first part of the body to touch the ball.
  On low balls get the hands near the ground — long legs make the bent posture
  hard, so coach the keeper down behind the ball. After bringing the ball in,
  **move forward with it** — this footwork is absolutely critical to "kill" the
  momentum of the shot.
- **Syllabus area:** [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 1)._

### Ball-Away-from-Body Catch

- **Objective:** Get the body behind a ball served to the side and finish the
  catch into the chest.
- **Setup:** Server delivers progressively wider of the keeper — first one
  step away, then two. Mark the keeper's start position.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) **First step is the most critical factor** —
  push off with the foot closest to the ball; ball to the keeper's right,
  **lead away** with the right foot. (2) If more than one step is needed, take
  additional steps to get the body behind the ball. (3) Finish the catch with
  several steps after the ball arrives to **lock** it into the chest.
- **Coaching cues:** The first step decides the technique — closest foot,
  toward the ball. Get the body behind it, not just the hands. The finishing
  steps lock the ball in.
- **Syllabus area:** [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md),
  [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 2)._

### Contour Catch

- **Objective:** Put two hands correctly behind the ball on a catch.
- **Setup:** Server delivers catchable balls at chest/head height; keeper in
  Ready Position.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Catch with **two hands behind the ball**,
  the index fingers and thumbs forming a **diamond** behind it. (2) Progress
  from static service to balls that require a small footwork adjustment first.
  (3) Combine with the **Basket Catch** so the keeper learns to bring the
  contour-caught ball into the body.
- **Coaching cues:** The diamond goes *behind* the ball, not on its sides —
  this mirrors Robo's concave-hands point. Two hands beat one every time.
- **Syllabus area:** [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL)._

### Basket Catch

- **Objective:** Securely catch the ball into the body using the torso as a
  barrier.
- **Setup:** Server delivers balls at the chest/stomach; keeper in Ready
  Position.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Create a **basket** with hands and arms and
  catch the ball into it, using the torso as a barrier behind the catch.
  (2) Progress from soft service to driven balls. (3) Add a forward step on
  contact (ties to Robo's "move forward to kill the momentum").
- **Coaching cues:** The torso is the backstop — if the hands miss, the body
  blocks. Curl/secure the ball in; no rebound out front.
- **Syllabus area:** [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL)._

### Scoop

- **Objective:** Securely collect a rolling ground ball.
- **Setup:** Server rolls balls along the ground to the keeper, then at slight
  angles. Survey the surface first — a bumpy ground makes this unsafe.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) It is a **Basket Catch from a roll on the
  ground** — let the ball **roll up and into the arms**. (2) Progress from
  straight rolls to balls rolling slightly to the side requiring a step first.
  (3) Add the forward finish to lock the ball in.
- **Coaching cues:** Get the hands behind and under as the ball arrives; let it
  roll up into the basket rather than slapping at it. Get the body behind the
  line of the ball.
- **Syllabus area:** [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL)._

### Three Hands

- **Objective:** Secure a ball to the ground with a safe hand structure on
  low/handling-to-ground saves.
- **Setup:** Server delivers low balls and balls that must be pinned on the
  ground (pairs naturally with the dive cards). Soft ground preferred.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Build the **three-hand system**: one hand
  **on top** of the ball, one hand **behind** the ball, and the **ground as the
  third hand** underneath. (2) Apply it to a ball pinned at full stretch on a
  dive landing. (3) Progress to live low service where the keeper must choose
  to scoop, basket, or pin with three hands.
- **Coaching cues:** Two hands plus the ground around the ball. On a full-
  stretch dive, the top hand goes on top of the ball and the bottom hand
  behind it; only pull the ball into the body once the landing is completely
  finished (Robo).
- **Syllabus area:** [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md),
  [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL); the pin-and-finish detail is Robo._

## Shot-Stopping & Diving

Cross-links: see [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md),
[footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md). All three dives are graded by **how far the ball
is from the body** (Plan/ECNL ladder; Robo's near-body vs. well-away detail).
Run them in order — they are a Simple-to-Complex ladder in themselves.

### Collapse Dive

- **Objective:** Make a short diving save for a ball served stiffly just to the
  side of the body.
- **Setup:** Keeper set; server delivers firm balls a half-stride to one side,
  low. Soft/even ground.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Take the **leg closest to the ball out from
  under yourself** so that foot collapses, sliding away from the ball, while
  the hands push to the ball simultaneously — the timing of both gets the
  keeper to the ground quickly. (2) End the dive with the ball pulled into the
  chest and the top leg pulled into the body. (3) Progress side to side and to
  firmer service.
- **Coaching cues:** Closest leg out, hands to the ball, *at the same time*.
  For a ball on the ground, **bend/flex the knee closest to the ball** when
  pushing off — a bent knee gets the body down to the ball. Land on the side;
  ball tucked into the chest.
- **Syllabus area:** [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL); near-body mechanics are Robo (technique 3)._

### Step Dive

- **Objective:** Make a medium-distance diving save.
- **Setup:** Keeper set; server delivers firm balls about one good step to
  either side. Cone the landing zone if useful.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Take **one good step with the foot closest
  to the ball** and dive, pushing the hands to the ball. (2) End with the ball
  pulled into the chest and the top leg pulled into the body. (3) Progress to
  rising balls and to either side off either foot.
- **Coaching cues:** One clean step off the near foot, then push. For a rising
  ball, push off the inside foot closest to the ball and push the hands across
  the body to a good handling position behind the ball (Robo). The dive is the
  response to a ball you can't reach upright — not the default.
- **Syllabus area:** [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL); step-and-dive mechanics are Robo (technique 3)._

### Extension / Power Dive

- **Objective:** Make a long diving save for a ball served well away from the
  body.
- **Setup:** Keeper set in a full goal; server delivers firm balls well wide,
  low and rising. Even, soft ground — this is the highest-impact landing.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Take a **long step with the foot closest to
  the ball**, follow with a **quick short step with the same foot**, and on
  that second short step **take off to dive**. (2) Using the **power step** —
  the top leg coming across the body as the keeper pushes off — spring toward
  the ball. (3) At full stretch, if needed, **pin the ball into the ground on
  landing** (top hand on top of the ball, bottom hand behind it), then pull the
  ball into the body **once the landing is completely finished**. (4) Progress
  to live/match service.
- **Coaching cues:** Long step, quick short step, take off — then the power
  step springs the body. Don't pull the ball in early; finish the landing
  first. Get hands and body behind the ball at full stretch.
- **Syllabus area:** [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md),
  [handling & catching](../syllabus/handling-catching.md).
- _Named drill (Plan / ECNL); extension mechanics + power step are Robo
  (technique 4)._

## Aerial & Crosses

Cross-links: see [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md),
[positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md). The same assessment strategy governs the high
ball and the cross: **hold position first to read speed, spin, and trajectory,
then go** (Robo). On every served ball, **claim or release verbally — "KEEPER"
or "AWAY" — every single time** (ECNL/Plan).

### High-Ball Catch (Catch at the Highest Point)

- **Objective:** Catch a ball served from distance with height, at a high
  point out in front of the body.
- **Setup:** Server delivers lofted balls into the goal area; goal and
  crossbar present. Progress to balls onto the crossbar and to balls under
  light pressure from a passive then active opponent.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) **Hold position** to assess the speed, spin,
  and trajectory before coming off the line. (2) Take a path that puts the
  keeper **in line with the ball**. (3) **Take off of one foot** and catch the
  ball **at a high point, out in front of the body**. (4) On absorbing it,
  **bend at the waist and move forward** to lock the ball into the body.
  (5) Progress to protecting the crossbar (next card) and to parrying over.
- **Coaching cues:** Assess before you move; the read is the technique. One-
  footed takeoff, catch high and in front. Bend at the waist to secure. The
  raised non-takeoff knee adds height and offers protection in a collision.
- **Syllabus area:** [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 5)._

### Protecting the Crossbar / Parry Over

- **Objective:** Defend a ball driven, chipped, or volleyed at the crossbar —
  catch on the line, or parry safely over.
- **Setup:** Server delivers balls at and just under the crossbar, some
  catchable, some that must go over. Keeper starts on or near the line.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Hold on the line to protect the crossbar —
  **even to the point of being back to the end line**. (2) When the ball is
  caught on the end line, hold it out in front of the body and move off the end
  line **"into" the ball**. (3) When the ball must be parried over: **open the
  palm and push underneath and through the back of the ball**. (4) Practice
  parrying with the **hand on the opposite side of the body** — a ball on the
  left side is parried with the right hand — as the safest play.
- **Coaching cues:** When in doubt, get it over the bar cleanly rather than
  risk a catch you can't hold. Opposite hand is often safest. Always pair with
  the verbal claim/release.
- **Syllabus area:** [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 5)._

### Cross from the Flank — Catch

- **Objective:** Read and catch a ball crossed from a flank, adjusting
  position as the ball travels.
- **Setup:** A wide server delivers crosses; mark near and far posts. Begin
  with the ball served straight at/in front of the keeper, then to the back
  post. Add teammates/opponents to introduce traffic.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) **Adjust the starting position as the ball
  moves out along the flank** — cover the near post but also cover ground
  toward the far post. (2) For a ball **straight at/in front** (the easiest),
  take a fairly straight path, **take off off one foot** (usually the foot to
  the inside of the goal), and let the **opposite foot/knee rise** to add
  height and protect in a collision. (3) For a ball **served past to the back
  post**, **track in a parallel line with it**. (4) To catch: come off the
  line, **plant on the inside foot, raise the outside knee, twist slightly** to
  put the palms in line with the ball; on contact, pull it into the chest and
  **continue forward, lowering the shoulders** — useful when contact with a
  teammate or opponent is likely.
- **Coaching cues:** Positioning is the first key — near post covered, ground
  covered to the far post. Assess like a high ball. Lower the shoulders through
  contact to protect the ball and ride a challenge.
- **Syllabus area:** [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 6)._

### Cross from the Flank — Punch (Two-Fist & One-Fist)

- **Objective:** Clear a contested cross when the keeper must come but cannot
  safely catch.
- **Setup:** Wide server delivers crosses into traffic (passive then active
  challengers). Goal present.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) When the keeper must come but will be
  challenged, choose between **two-fisted punch** (to direct the ball back
  where it came from) and **one-fisted punch** (to continue the ball on its
  way). (2) Drill the two-fisted punch first for reliability. (3) Add a
  challenger so the keeper learns to punch *through* contact.
- **Coaching cues:** The **two-fisted punch is generally the more reliable
  punch — more surface area behind the ball**. Catch first whenever possible;
  punch is the choice only when the catch isn't safe (Robo). Punch up, out, and
  away from danger.
- **Syllabus area:** [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 6)._

### Corner-Kick Management

- **Objective:** Organize marking on a corner and decide when to attack the
  ball in the air.
- **Setup:** Full goal, corner taker, post players, marked runners, and
  attackers. Mark the green **"Go" area** in front of/around the keeper. Drill
  both in-swingers and out-swingers.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Before the kick, the keeper **commands
  quickly for posts and marked runners** and makes sure **no opponent is left
  unmarked**. (2) The keeper **watches for a short corner**, demanding field
  players chase immediately. (3) **Post players do NOT leave until the keeper
  tells them to push up.** (4) On the serve, the keeper steps into the **"Go"
  area — the keeper's land to step out and win balls in the air** — and calls
  **"KEEPER!"** to claim or **"AWAY!"** to release, **every single time**.
  (5) Progress to live, contested corners where teams challenge the keeper
  early.
- **Coaching cues:** Be confident and aggressive — expect teams to challenge
  the keeper early to force decisions. Read the swing: **out-swinger** = ball
  more likely to swing **out away from you**; **in-swinger** = ball more likely
  to swing **into you**. Claim or release verbally without fail.
- **Syllabus area:** [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [communication](../framework/communication-syllabus.md).
- _Named drill (Plan); diagram-based in ECNL._

## One-v-One & Breakaway

Cross-links: see [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md),
[positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md). The keeper's true job here is to **delay and buy
time for teammates to recover** — winning the ball outright is one option, not
the mandate (ECNL/Plan).

### Dive at the Feet (1v1 Smother)

- **Objective:** Win the ball at an oncoming opponent's feet on a through ball.
- **Setup:** A server/dribbler runs onto a ball played into space; keeper
  starts off the line. Soft ground.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Come off the line to deal with the through
  ball — it is like a simple low-ball dive. (2) **Push off the foot nearest the
  ball.** (3) Make the **decision to dive when the ball is played away from the
  oncoming opponent**. (4) Spread and **press the hands into the ground** to
  hold the ball if challenged. (5) After the opponent goes by, pull the ball
  into the chest.
- **Coaching cues:** Time the dive to the moment the ball leaves the
  attacker's foot. **Keep the top elbow up** to protect the head. **Do NOT
  pull the top leg across the body too early** — that pushes the shoulders
  backward. Hands pin the ball to the ground.
- **Syllabus area:** [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md),
  [shot-stopping & diving](../syllabus/shot-stopping-diving.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 7)._

### Breakaway Decision / Meet at the Highest Point

- **Objective:** Manage a 1v1 breakaway — delay, protect against the chip, and
  meet the ball at the highest point.
- **Setup:** Attacker dribbles at goal from distance; keeper starts on/near the
  line. Use the diagram's four positions: attacker 1st→4th paired with keeper
  1st→4th. Vary the dribbler's speed.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) As the attacker advances, the keeper
  **advances through 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th positions** to **meet the opponent
  at the highest point possible**. (2) **Steal ground forward only once the
  opponent dribbles the ball away from their foot.** (3) **Keep the feet as
  long as possible** — delay to let teammates recover. (4) Protect the chip
  throughout. (5) Progress to live breakaways at varied speeds, then add a
  recovering defender so delay pays off.
- **Coaching cues:** **The chip is the most important worry in the beginning** —
  protect it first. Meet at the highest point *without giving up the chip*.
  **Rule of Thumb: the faster a player is dribbling, the more likely they are
  to try to beat the keeper 1v1; as a player slows down, they prepare for the
  shot.** The true job is to delay and allow teammates to recover and help.
- **Syllabus area:** [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md).
- _Named drill (Plan); diagram-based in ECNL._

### Through-Ball Decision

- **Objective:** Read a ball played through the defense and choose to win it or
  hold.
- **Setup:** A ball is played through the back line into space; an opponent
  makes a through-run onto it. Keeper starts at a realistic line height. Add
  backside runners.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) The keeper **reads the ball** and decides
  whether they can **win it** OR **steal ground and stalk**. (2) If the keeper
  decides to go out, they have **committed and must stick to the decision**.
  (3) If not, the keeper decides whether to **step and smother** or **steal
  ground / stalk and prepare for the shot or 1v1**. (4) Add **backside
  opponents making runs through the seams** so the keeper learns to scan.
- **Coaching cues:** Be aware of backside opponents finding the seams. Commit
  fully or hold fully — the half-decision is what gets punished. Once out,
  stay out.
- **Syllabus area:** [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md).
- _Named drill (Plan); diagram-based in ECNL._

### "Can I Win the Ball?" Decision (Hold / Re-Angle / Win)

- **Objective:** Train the come-or-stay decision on through balls and crosses,
  with the verbal commitment that goes with it.
- **Setup:** A server plays balls into the area at varied distances with
  teammates and opponents present. Keeper reads each and chooses a response.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) On each ball the keeper runs the question
  **"Can I win the ball?"** — weighing **total distance to travel, whether a
  teammate is closer, and obstacles in the path**. (2) The keeper chooses:
  **hold**, **come part way and take a new angle and hold**, or **come all the
  way and win it outright**. (3) When committing to come win it, the keeper
  calls **"KEEPER!"** so teammates take cover and opponents know a challenge is
  coming. (4) Progress to live service so the decision is made under match
  speed.
- **Coaching cues:** "Can I win the ball?" is a **consideration, not a
  commandment**. Catch is safest; a punched/parried ball can keep the team
  under pressure — catch first, parry/punch as a last resort. The "KEEPER!"
  call is a command of courtesy and tactics.
- **Syllabus area:** [one-v-one & breakaway](../syllabus/one-v-one-breakaway.md),
  [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [communication](../framework/communication-syllabus.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (positional play)._

## Positioning & Angles

Cross-links: see [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
[footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md).

### Angle Play — Near Post First, Then Far

- **Objective:** Take the correct angle as the ball moves, prioritizing the
  near post.
- **Setup:** Mark a starting position **1–2 yards off the end line** for when
  the ball is in the final third. Servers move the ball around the final third;
  keeper adjusts and then a shot comes.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) From the starting position, react to the
  ball's movement: **ball straight toward goal → come straight off the line**;
  **ball moving square across the field → move across the goal similarly**;
  **ball toward goal but angled away → move across AND up at an angle to narrow
  the angle**. (2) Recognize the two final-third situations: can I **move
  forward to narrow the angle (set 4, 5, or 6 yards from goal)?** or must I
  **consider a driven/volleyed/chipped serve to the crossbar** and how long it
  takes to recover? (3) End each rep **set** for the shot.
- **Coaching cues:** **Coverage of the near post is the first priority; the
  second (always balanced with the first) is the right angle to get across to
  the far post.** Central shot → central position; ball from the right →
  position tempered to the right. Always know how far you are from the
  crossbar.
- **Syllabus area:** [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [footwork & movement](../syllabus/footwork-movement.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (positional play)._

### Stand Up & Hold (React, Don't Anticipate)

- **Objective:** Hold a set position and react to the shot rather than
  anticipating it.
- **Setup:** Keeper set at a realistic distance; server strikes on goal from
  16–24 yards, varying timing and placement so the keeper cannot guess.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Just prior to the shot, the only response is
  to **stand up, hold a position, and react quickly when the ball is played.**
  (2) When the ball is **away from goal (16–24 yards)**: upright, shoulders
  rolled forward, hands hanging down and slightly tense (not stiff), feet about
  shoulder width, weight on the **balls of the feet**. (3) When **close (3, 4,
  5 yards) or off the line**: hold with knees slightly bent and hands low to
  protect a shot from going under. (4) Server deliberately varies release so
  anticipation is punished and patience rewarded.
- **Coaching cues:** **Anticipating what might happen before it happens usually
  leads to failure**; holding and then reacting gives a good chance to react
  positively. Developing this takes years. A developed keeper, asked what they
  think about when set, answers **"absolutely nothing."**
- **Syllabus area:** [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [psychology & mentality](../syllabus/psychology-mentality.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (positional play)._

## Distribution & Playing With Feet

Cross-links: see [distribution](../syllabus/distribution.md),
[playing with feet](../syllabus/playing-with-feet.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md). Robo's guiding rule for developing keepers:
**"when in doubt, kick it out."** Punting and dropkicking **take several years
and many matches to get a good feel for** — expect slow progress.

### Punt

- **Objective:** Kick a ball out of the hand for distance with a high, arching
  flight.
- **Setup:** Open space; targets at distance. Keeper with a ball in hand.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Build momentum: start with a **long step**
  then take **several shorter steps** to the contact point (young keepers may
  find holding the ball with both hands easier than moving with one). (2)
  **Plant the non-kicking foot, leg slightly bent.** (3) Move the ball forward
  on a plane away from the body at **about hip height or just under**, hold it
  steady, then **drop it in front of the body**. (4) **Look at the ball; make
  contact behind and underneath it**; the **kicking foot pointed**; come
  forward onto the kicking foot. (5) Progress to targets and to punting under
  light time pressure.
- **Coaching cues:** Good result: a **high, arching ball that "turns over" in
  mid-flight** and extends its distance. Eyes on the ball through contact.
- **Syllabus area:** [distribution](../syllabus/distribution.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 8)._

### Dropkick

- **Objective:** Kick a ball out of the hand on a low, driven trajectory.
- **Setup:** Open space; targets at distance. Keeper with a ball in hand.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Same initial run-up as the punt; **plant the
  non-kicking foot**, **shoulder comes forward**, the **hand holding the ball
  gets close to the ground**. (2) **Drop the ball**, **lean the shoulders
  backward**, and the **kicking foot immediately comes through the back of the
  ball** with the **toes locked down**. (3) The ball is **driven on a low
  trajectory**. (4) Progress to targets and to a moving/recovering teammate.
- **Coaching cues:** The lower the intended delivery, the more the non-kicking
  leg flexes and the lower the shoulders get to the ground. Toes locked, drive
  through the back of the ball.
- **Syllabus area:** [distribution](../syllabus/distribution.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 8)._

### Throwing for Distance & Accuracy

- **Objective:** Throw the ball accurately onto a teammate, including a moving
  target.
- **Setup:** A target teammate (static, then moving); markers for distance.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Take **steps toward the target**; **plant to
  throw with the knee of the planting foot bent**. (2) **Prepare the ball
  behind the body, palm up.** (3) As the shoulders come forward the throwing
  arm comes forward; **as the ball leaves the hand the palm "opens" — palm and
  fingers facing down.** (4) Progress to **throwing onto the foot of a moving
  teammate.**
- **Coaching cues:** The ball should leave the hand directly, **without a lot
  of side-ways spin**. The lower the delivery, the more the non-throwing leg
  flexes and the lower the shoulders. **Throws with a slight loft that bounce
  in front of or to the side of a teammate are very difficult to control** —
  aim for the foot, flat and direct.
- **Syllabus area:** [distribution](../syllabus/distribution.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 10)._

### Backpass — Kicking a Moving Ball Off the Ground

- **Objective:** Receive and clear or play a ball passed back along the ground
  by a teammate.
- **Setup:** A teammate plays balls back to the keeper — smooth passes and
  bouncing ground balls; add an opponent who contests the backpass.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) **Hold position as the ball is played back**
  to assess pressure on the backpass and the quality of the pass; holding lets
  minor adjustments be made as the ball arrives. (2) **Meet the ball early if
  the backpass is being contested.** (3) Play **smooth backpasses with the
  instep, driven away**; play balls that **come in on the ground and bounce
  with the side of the foot.** (4) Progress to live pressure and to a first
  decision: clear or play out.
- **Coaching cues:** Read pressure first, then choose the surface. **"When in
  doubt, kick it out."** As the keeper matures, develop sound passing from deep
  positions — but safety first while developing.
- **Syllabus area:** [distribution](../syllabus/distribution.md),
  [playing with feet](../syllabus/playing-with-feet.md).
- _Technique-implied from Robo (technique 9)._

## Set Pieces (Free Kicks & Walls)

Cross-links: see [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md),
[positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
[communication](../framework/communication-syllabus.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md). On every set piece, **claim or release the ball
verbally — "KEEPER" or "AWAY" — no matter what, every single time** (including
balls that fly well over the goal).

### Free-Kick Wall Setup / Body Shape

- **Objective:** Set the wall and position correctly for a direct free kick.
- **Setup:** Free-kick spots at varied locations; a shooter (or two), wall
  players, and a server. Mark ten yards from the ball.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) The keeper sets **body shape square with the
  ball and goal.** (2) Call **"WALL (#)"** — players form ten yards from goal —
  then the keeper **moves the wall into position**, picking which side the wall
  covers (the keeper must know their own strengths). (3) **The tallest player
  on the end of the wall is closest to the post the wall is covering.** (4)
  **At least one player steps in front of the ball** to make the shooter wait
  for the whistle; **put a teammate to the side of the wall if two or more
  shooters are at the ball.** (5) The keeper **plans to dive to cover the
  over-the-wall area.** (6) Progress to live free kicks where the keeper reads
  the shooter.
- **Coaching cues:** **The wall doesn't stop people from scoring near post —
  the keeper must plan to dive that way to cover that area.** **People love to
  shoot over the wall**, so the wall's job is to make the shot difficult.
  **Watch the shooter's body shape — sometimes shooters give away which side
  they want to go.** If the shooter is good at free kicks, maybe more players in
  the wall. Claim or release every ball verbally.
- **Syllabus area:** [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [aerial & crosses](../syllabus/aerial-crosses.md),
  [communication](../framework/communication-syllabus.md).
- _Named drill (Plan); diagram-based in ECNL._

### Wall Numbers ("How Many Go Into the Wall?")

- **Objective:** Decide how many players go in the wall by the spot/zone of the
  free kick.
- **Setup:** Mark free-kick spots across the colored zones of the diagram, from
  wide flanks to central positions just outside the box. A wall and a shooter
  at each.
- **Execution / progression:** Walk the keeper through each zone and call the
  recommended wall size, **closer and more central = bigger wall:** **1** in the
  wide zones (both flanks); **2**, then **3**, then **4** in progressively more
  central/closer zones; **5** around the top of the box; **6–8** and **8–10** in
  the closest central zones directly in front of goal. Repeat until the keeper
  calls the number instantly from any spot.
- **Coaching cues:** It is a map, not a rule book — the central/close end gets
  the big walls because that is where the direct shot is most dangerous; the
  wide end gets one player because the angle is poor. If the shooter is
  especially good, bias upward.
- **Syllabus area:** [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [communication](../framework/communication-syllabus.md).
- _Named drill (Plan); the heat-map is in both Plan and ECNL ("How Many Go
  Into The Wall?"). Note: Plan and ECNL describe the same zones; the exact
  top-bracket label appears as "8–10" (Plan) / "9–10" (ECNL) — both mean "most
  of the team" for the closest central free kicks._

### Indirect Free Kick Inside the Box

- **Objective:** Defend an indirect free kick awarded against the keeper inside
  their own box.
- **Setup:** Set up the indirect free kick scenario inside the six/eighteen;
  attackers (one to touch, one to shoot), the keeper's full team for a wall.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) Establish *why* it's awarded — the keeper,
  inside their own box, committed one of: **held the ball with hands more than
  six seconds**; **touched it again with hands after releasing it before
  another player touched it**; **touched with hands after it was deliberately
  kicked to them by a teammate**; **touched with hands after receiving it
  directly from a teammate's throw-in.** (2) Build a **wall covering as much of
  the goal as possible.** (3) Because it is **indirect it must be touched
  first** — on that first touch the **keeper charges the ball to cut the angle
  and stop the shot, while the wall stays behind to cover.** (4) If the keeper
  must take it after conceding, **kill the play — kick the ball as far as
  possible to force a whistle (accept a yellow card if needed).**
- **Coaching cues:** Two jobs: wall covers the goal, keeper attacks the toucher.
  **Kill the play** is the governing instruction — distance buys the whistle.
  Knowing the four offences is the prevention half of the drill.
- **Syllabus area:** [positioning & angles](../syllabus/positioning-angles.md),
  [distribution](../syllabus/distribution.md),
  [communication](../framework/communication-syllabus.md).
- _Named drill (Plan); diagram-based in ECNL._

## Communication (Run Inside Every Drill)

Cross-links: see the [communication syllabus](../framework/communication-syllabus.md),
[psychology & mentality](../syllabus/psychology-mentality.md), and the
[glossary](../vocabulary.md). The sources give no standalone communication
*drills* — communication is layered onto the situational cards above. Use the
four-stage progression as the rule for *what* to add as the keeper matures.

### Verbal Claim / Release ("KEEPER" / "AWAY")

- **Objective:** Make the two-syllable claim or release on every ball, every
  time.
- **Setup:** Bolt this onto any service, cross, corner, or set-piece card
  above. A server delivers a mix of catchable, releasable, and over-the-goal
  balls.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) On every ball the keeper can save or handle,
  call **"KEEPER"** (always **two syllables** — never allow simply yelling
  "Keep"). (2) On every ball the keeper cannot make or wants a teammate to take,
  call **"AWAY."** (3) Require the call **no matter what, every single time** —
  including balls that fly well over the goal. (4) Add teammates so the call
  has a real audience.
- **Coaching cues:** Loud, clear, concise. Tone carries urgency or calmness —
  the keeper is always in control. **A goalkeeper is never a cheerleader** —
  deliver helpful information, don't just shout common terms.
- **Syllabus area:** [communication syllabus](../framework/communication-syllabus.md),
  [psychology & mentality](../syllabus/psychology-mentality.md).
- _Named in ECNL/Plan (Communication Stage One)._

### Organizing Commands (Stages Two–Four)

- **Objective:** Layer the right organizing command onto a situational drill as
  the keeper's scope grows.
- **Setup:** Run the corner, free-kick, breakaway, or through-ball cards above
  with the keeper's defenders present so commands have targets.
- **Execution / progression:** (1) **Stage Two — individual teammate:** e.g.
  **"(NAME) STEP/PRESSURE," "FORCE LEFT/RIGHT," "(NAME) DROP," "MAN ON,"
  "TIME," "(KEEPER'S) HOME."** (2) **Stage Three — the back four:** e.g.
  **"DROP BACK," "PUSH UP," "CONTAIN/DELAY," "WALL (#)," "HOLD (LINE/SPOT/
  NAME)," "(NAME) MARK (#)," "GOAL SIDE."** (3) **Stage Four — whole team:**
  e.g. **"SWITCH," "SWING," "(NAME) RECOVER CENTRAL," "(NAME) BACK POST,"
  "INTO TOUCH," "SHIFT (RIGHT/LEFT)."** (4) Match the stage to the keeper's
  age band — Communication enters the syllabus at U13–U15 and becomes Advanced
  Communication at U16–U18.
- **Coaching cues:** Louder voice expresses more urgency. Commands assist
  attacking moves too, not only defending. Full term definitions live in the
  [communication syllabus](../framework/communication-syllabus.md) and the
  [glossary](../vocabulary.md).
- **Syllabus area:** [communication syllabus](../framework/communication-syllabus.md).
- _Named in ECNL/Plan (Communication Stages Two–Four)._

## Sources

This drill library synthesizes all three source documents:

- **Plan** — *Goalkeeper Plan 2015–2016* — the primary source for named
  drills: the footwork sequence (Stalk, Steal Ground, Power Step), the dive
  family (Collapse / Step / Extension-Power), the catch family (Basket Catch,
  Scoop, Contour Catch, Three Hands), and the set-piece / 1v1 situations
  (Breakaway Decision, Through-Ball Decision, Free-Kick Wall Setup, Wall
  Numbers, Corner-Kick Management, Indirect Free Kick Inside the Box). It is
  the only one of the three with an explicit drill section.
- **ECNL** — *ECNL Goalkeeping Curriculum & Handbook, First Edition* (BUSA) —
  parallel definitions of the same named techniques and the four-stage
  communication terms, plus the diagram-based set-piece and breakaway rules
  ("Go" area, in-/out-swinger, meet at the highest point, kill the play).
- **Robo** — *"The GK Trainer," Developing a Training Program for Coaching
  Goalkeeping* (Rob Walker, US National Goalkeeping Staff) — supplies the
  technique-implied cards. Robo has no drill section, so its ten techniques
  (ball-at-body and away-from-body catching, near-body and well-away diving,
  high balls, crosses/punching, dive at feet, punt, dropkick, throwing,
  backpass) are written up here as cards with Robo's own coaching detail; no
  technique or drill outside the sources has been invented.

See [`../reference/`](../reference/README.md) for the source PDFs.
